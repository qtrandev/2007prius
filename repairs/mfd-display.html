📺 Main Display Screen Goes Dark

Car drives fine but the MFD screen is off. The fix is a single fuse reset.

🟢 Easy ⏱ 2 Minutes 💰 Free 🔧 No tools needed
Home › Repairs › MFD Screen Goes Dark

Symptoms

  • The main Multi-Function Display (MFD) touchscreen goes completely dark
  • The car starts and drives normally — no warning lights, no hybrid system issues
  • The display does not come back on its own after a restart
  • Audio, climate, and navigation controls on the screen are unresponsive
If the car still drives fine, this is almost certainly the DOME fuse. A display failure that takes the whole car with it is a different problem. If you have normal power, normal hybrid operation, and just a dark screen — try this fix first.

The Fix: Reset Jumper 15 (DOME Fuse)

The MFD screen is on the same fuse circuit as the interior dome light — fuse slot 15, labeled DOME, in the interior fuse box. When this fuse trips or the circuit glitches, the display loses power while everything else continues working normally.

The fix is pulling the fuse to reset the circuit. No replacement fuse needed unless the fuse itself is blown.

Step-by-Step

  1. Turn the car off and remove the key (or press Power to off).
  2. Locate the interior fuse box. On the Gen 2 Prius it's on the driver's side, behind a small panel on the left end of the dashboard — pull the panel cover off to reveal it.
  3. Find fuse slot 15, labeled DOME. The fuse box cover has a diagram showing which slot is which. Slot 15 / DOME is a small blade fuse.
  4. Pull the fuse out using the fuse puller tool stored in the fuse box (or needle-nose pliers). Inspect it — the metal strip inside should be intact. If it's broken, the fuse is blown and needs replacing (same amperage).
  5. Wait 10–15 seconds, then push the fuse firmly back in.
  6. Start the car. The MFD screen should power on normally during startup.
If the screen goes dark again shortly after, there may be a short circuit or a failing display unit drawing too much current and blowing the fuse repeatedly. At that point the display unit itself may need replacement — a junkyard MFD from a Gen 2 donor car is the practical fix.

About the Gen 2 MFD Screen

The Multi-Function Display is a touch-sensitive LCD screen in the center of the dashboard. It controls:

  • Audio / navigation
  • Climate control settings
  • Hybrid system energy flow display
  • Fuel economy history
  • Vehicle settings

On high-mileage Gen 2 cars the touchscreen calibration can also drift — taps register in the wrong spot. This is a separate issue from the screen going dark and is typically fixed by recalibration through the service menu, or by replacing the unit.

Notes from the Field

The DOME fuse reset is one of those fixes that takes 2 minutes and feels almost too simple for a problem that looks serious. A completely dark center screen on a modern car feels catastrophic — but if the car drives normally, it almost always comes down to this one fuse circuit.

This fix also works if the interior dome light stops working at the same time as the display — they're on the same circuit, which is the confirmation that you're dealing with the right fuse.

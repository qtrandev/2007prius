🔑 Key Fob Battery Replacement

The smart key dies every few years. CR2032 battery, $3 at any pharmacy. And if the battery is completely dead, the car can still start — there's a trick for that too.

🟢 Easy ⏱ 5 Minutes 💰 $3–$5 (CR2032 battery) 📅 Every 3–5 Years
Home › Repairs › Key Fob Battery

Symptoms of a Weak Key Fob Battery

  • "Smart key battery low" warning on the Multi-Function Display (MFD)
  • Reduced range — you have to be right next to the car for it to detect the key
  • Door doesn't unlock when you approach or touch the handle
  • The POWER button doesn't respond normally even when you're inside with the fob
  • Intermittent detection — sometimes works, sometimes doesn't
Don't wait for the "key battery low" warning. That message means the battery is already struggling. Replace it when you see it — waiting until it's completely dead means dealing with the emergency start procedure in a parking lot.

What Battery You Need

CR2032 — a 3V lithium coin cell. This is one of the most common coin batteries made. Available at any pharmacy (Walgreens, CVS), hardware store, grocery store, AutoZone, Walmart, or online. Costs $3–5 for a single, or less if bought in a multi-pack. Any brand works — Panasonic, Energizer, Duracell, and generic all perform similarly in this application.

How to Open the Key Fob

The Gen 2 Prius uses a Smart Key fob — a black rectangular remote with a hidden mechanical emergency key inside. Opening it takes about 30 seconds once you know the method.

  1. Release the mechanical key. On the back of the fob, there's a small sliding tab or button (usually on one of the short ends). Press or slide it and pull out the thin metal key that's tucked inside. Set the key aside — you don't need it for this but you will need it later.
  2. Find the seam where the key was. With the metal key removed, there's now a slot at the end of the fob where the key came out. This is your pry point.
  3. Insert a coin or small flathead screwdriver into that slot and gently twist to separate the two halves of the case. Work slowly — the plastic tabs are small but not fragile if you're not forcing it. Don't use a sharp metal tool along the outer seam; you'll scratch the case and risk cracking a clip.
  4. The two halves will separate. Inside you'll see the circuit board and the CR2032 battery in a holder. Note which side of the battery faces up (the "+" side is marked on the battery and typically faces up).
  5. Pop out the old battery using a fingernail or the corner of a flathead. Slide in the new CR2032 with the same orientation — positive side up.
  6. Snap the two halves back together — press firmly along the seam until all the clips click. Reinsert the mechanical key until it locks in place.
  7. Test it — walk away from the car and press Lock or Unlock. If the car responds, you're done.
Don't pry along the outer edge of the fob. The seam clips are inside, not at the very edge. Jamming a tool into the outer seam can crack the case. The slot where the emergency key was is the correct pry point.

Emergency Start — If the Battery Is Completely Dead

If the fob battery dies while you're out and you don't have a spare, the Prius can still be started. Toyota built this in specifically for this situation.

  1. Get inside the car. If the door handle doesn't work (no battery to transmit), use the mechanical key hidden inside the fob to unlock the driver's door manually. The keyhole is behind a small cover on the door handle.
  2. Once inside, hold the key fob directly against the POWER button. Touch the top or bottom edge of the fob to the button. There's an antenna coil behind the button that can read the fob's transponder passively — no battery needed for this short-range read.
  3. With the fob held against the button, press the POWER button with your foot on the brake as usual. The car should start normally.
This works because the fob has a passive RFID transponder that doesn't need battery power — it only needs to be close enough for the car's antenna to read it. The battery powers the wireless buttons (Lock/Unlock/range detection), not the transponder itself.

Notes from the Field

The "smart key battery low" message on the MFD is easy to ignore for a few weeks — it's not urgent-sounding and the fob keeps working. Then one day it stops detecting the key from your pocket entirely and you're standing next to the car wondering if the car has a problem.

The CR2032 is the same battery used in countless other remotes, watches, and devices. Buying a two-pack keeps a spare in the glovebox for the next time. Replacement takes less than five minutes and no tools beyond a coin.

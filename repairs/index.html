Repair Guides

Real fixes from real ownership. Difficulty, cost, and parts — all in one place.

🟢 Easy — Anyone can do it 🟡 Medium — Some experience needed 🔴 Hard — Research thoroughly first

Hybrid & Cooling System

⚡

Hybrid Battery Replacement

Red triangle, HV battery warning, power loss. The biggest repair most Gen 2 owners will face — but it's not always as expensive as you think.

Hard
💧

Inverter Coolant Pump

One of the best preventive repairs you can do. This $80 pump keeps your inverter alive. Replacing it proactively costs less than one hour of tow fees.

Easy
🌡️

P1121 — Coolant Flow Control Valve

No cabin heat and a check engine light. The three-way coolant valve sticks on high-mileage cars. Best done alongside the inverter pump replacement — separate coolant circuits, but the same physical access area and the same headlight removal step.

Medium

Brakes

🛑

ABS Actuator

ABS, VSC, and brake lights all on at once — the "3 amigos." The actuator pump runs constantly and drains the battery. Dealer wants $1,500. There are better options.

Hard

Electrical & Instrument

🎛️

Combination Meter (Instrument Cluster)

Speedometer drops to 0 while driving. Fuel gauge stuck. Odometer frozen. A capacitor repair can save you $400+ over dealer replacement.

Medium
📺

MFD Screen — Fuse Reset & Replacement

Screen goes dark: try the DOME fuse first (free, 5 min). Still broken? Full physical replacement guide including compatibility, delamination risk, and the vent clips that always snap.

Easy–Medium

Maintenance

🔋

12V Auxiliary Battery

When it dies, the car is completely dead — no dash, no locks, nothing. Replace every 3–5 years (sooner in hot climates). Also covers disconnecting to reset error codes and surviving long trips.

Easy
🔩

Spark Plug Replacement

Scheduled at 120,000 miles (Toyota US official). Iridium plugs only — the hybrid system starts and stops the engine constantly, and cheap plugs don't survive the extra ignition cycles. OEM part numbers and torque specs included.

Medium
🛢️

Oil Consumption

At 150k+ miles, the engine starts burning oil between changes. Knocking and weak acceleration are the symptoms — but by then it's already low. Check the dipstick monthly.

Easy
🌬️

Cabin Air Filter

No tools, $10–15, done in 10 minutes. Most owners don't know it exists until it's been 50,000 miles. Behind the glovebox.

Easy
🔑

Key Fob Battery

CR2032, $3 at any pharmacy. How to open the fob without cracking the case — and how to start the car if the battery is already completely dead.

Easy

Comfort & Climate

❄️

AC Recharge — Adding Refrigerant

AC stops blowing cold after 10+ years. A $20 R-134a can from Walmart buys more time. Covers the correct low-side procedure, what the Prius electric compressor changes, and what diminishing results mean.

Easy

Diagnostics

📟

Clear Engine Fault Codes (OBD2)

A $15 Bluetooth adapter and a free app is all you need. Step-by-step guide including the P0420 catalytic converter code that shows up every few months on high-mileage Priuses.

Easy
💨

P0420 — Catalyst System Efficiency

The most common check engine light on high-mileage Priuses. Rarely an emergency. Covers diagnosis, O2 sensor vs. cat replacement, costs, and catalytic converter theft.

Medium
⛽

Fuel Gauge Inaccuracy

Gauge stays full too long then drops suddenly, or the pump clicks off after 5 gallons. The rubber bladder fuel tank is a known Gen 2 design flaw. Free calibration reset to try first — tank replacement at $1,000+ if it doesn't help.

Medium

Lighting

💡

Headlight Bulb Replacement

No tools needed. The push-down-then-forward trick everyone misses the first time. Bulb recommendations and YouTube guides.

Easy

Tires

🏎️

Tire Replacements

17 years, 250,000+ miles, six sets of tires. Real costs, real mileage, and a full price comparison from South Florida ownership.

Easy

Body & Exterior

🔒

Rear Hatch Latch

Hatch won't open, won't stay closed, or triggers false alarms overnight. The latch assembly fails at high mileage — trim panel off, swap the unit, done.

Medium
🔨

Hatch Lift Struts

When the hatch falls on your head while loading cargo, the gas struts are dead. A $30 fix that takes 20 minutes.

Easy
🌧️

Trunk Water Leak

Water in the cargo area usually comes from three places at once: tail light gaskets, roof weld seams, or hatch weatherstripping. Fix the right one before the spare tire well rusts through.

Medium
🚙

Window Weatherstrip

The rubber falls off after 10 years of Florida sun. All 4 OEM part numbers in one place — because the original video guide disappeared from the internet.

Easy
